Mandaragiri Hills, Tumkur

About Mandaragiri Hills

Mandaragiri Hills, located in Tumkur, is a serene destination offering breathtaking views and historical significance. Known for its picturesque landscapes and Jain heritage, it is an ideal spot for both trekking enthusiasts and spiritual seekers. The hilltop features a series of ancient Jain temples and a towering monolithic statue of Chandraprabha, adding to its religious and architectural charm. The highlight of Mandaragiri Hills is the Guru Mandir, an 81-feet temple dedicated to Acharya Shantisagar, designed in the shape of a peacock feather. This unique structure, often called the Peacock Temple, is a symbol of peace and Jain philosophy. Visitors can also explore Maidala Kere and Padmavati Kere, two scenic lakes that enhance the tranquility of the site. The lush greenery and fresh air make this place a perfect retreat for nature lovers and devotees alike.
